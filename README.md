## Minigame Overviews
*Paragraphs written by Rune and I to describe each minigame to the contestants before they are commenced. These are not intended as proper descriptions of each game, but rather as rule explanations to people who are mostly already familiar with the basic concepts.*

### Battle Box
In Battle Box, teams win by filling in the 3x3 wool centre with their team’s coloured wool and preventing the opposing team from doing so, whether that be through PvP or sneaky tactics. 
At the beginning of each round, you will drop into the arena and have 10 seconds of mining fatigue. In this period, it is smart to collect the randomly spawned items around the map, as they can swing the outcome of not only a fight, but also a round if used properly. 
Given the strategic nature of this game, we have provided you with private team channels to facilitate better communication and discussion of strategies. 
Every team will play each other once.
And lastly, points are awarded for kills and for filling in the centre.

### Boat Race
This game is pretty self-explanatory.
You are given 4 points for every player you are ahead of after a lap, and so it is crucial to be fast and avoid hitting walls and other players, as Minecraft’s logic surrounding boats can be finicky, and often leads to complete losses of momentum.
While some routes may be “faster”, they may be slower for this reason.
Teams with more points will start further back.

### Glass
For this game, please note that there is a jump cooldown, lasting around 5 seconds. This is to make sure that no one can hop, skip, and jump their way to the end without risk or comedic suspense.
Do keep this in mind, and stay patient.
If you are not chosen to move by the game, as found in the chat, you do not have to jump, and you can let other players make progress for you.
There are 3 rounds, and teams will be chosen to move in order of highest to lowest points, which may change after each round, so keep an eye out for which players are being chosen in the chat.

### One Shot
In this very fast-paced game, the premise is simple. It takes only 1 arrow to kill, or die.
So, to get a high amount of points, you must get as many kills as possible over the 10 minutes, as each kill is worth 3 points.
Do note, however, that respawning players are given temporary spawn protection and will not have that glowing outline that killable players will.

### Parkour
Parkour is pretty simple. There are 33 levels to be completed within 15 minutes. If you're having a hard time with a level, right-click the remote to skip to the next uncompleted level. A quick example would be if you are at the end of the parkour, but did not complete levels 6, 7, or 21. Right-clicking the remote in this example would take you straight to level 6, and you can continue as normal.

### Spleef
In spleef, you must dig out blocks underneath other players to be the last player or team left. For every ten snow blocks that you mine, you will receive an exploding snowball. These snowballs cannot hit players and instead can be thrown at snowblocks to destroy them. They are most effective when thrown at the centre of the side of a block. You gain 3 points for every player you outlast, and there are 7 rounds.

### Sumo
In sumo, you must knock other players into the void to win. For every player you outlast, you gain 2 points. You cannot hit teammates. Everyone will start with a stick enchanted with knockback 1, which will increase in strength every 30 seconds, to a maximum of level 3. Additionally, wind charges will be given every 7s, as long as you do not already have one, so it is smart to use them soon after you get them. They are super useful for gaining height by throwing them at your feet and jumping at the same time, or they can be thrown at others to knock them slightly.
There will be 2 rounds per map.
Be mindful of heights, as a death barrier will slowly lower over the course of every round.
Its position and your relative safety can be found just above your hotbar.  
When entering the barrier, there is a sound indicator, and you will have 5 seconds to descend; otherwise, you will die.

### TNT-Tag
For TNT-Tag, there are 3 rounds. From the start of each round, 2 players will be randomly selected every 30 seconds and be given TNT. These players are taggers, and they must tag other players to pass the TNT off to not explode at the end of the timer. Furthermore, taggers have speed and jump boosts. After tagging someone, you are given a brief boost to these stats to help you get away.
For every player you outlast, you are given 7 points.
Note that you cannot tag your teammates.
To allow you guys to learn the maps and some potential escape routes, when the honey-door opens, you will have some free-running time to explore before TNT is given.
